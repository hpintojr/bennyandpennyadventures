Kid-Friendly Glossary

♥

Plain-language definitions you can read aloud together.

Infusion

Medicine that flows slowly into the body through a tiny tube — like a gentle drink for the inside.

Port

A small button placed under the skin that gives nurses an easy, comfy spot to give medicine.

PICC line

A soft, bendy line that goes in the arm so medicine can travel right where it's needed.

Central line

A special line that reaches a big vein near the heart to deliver medicine or collect labs.

IV

A tiny tube placed in a vein to give fluids or medicine.

MRI

A big machine that takes pictures of the inside of the body using magnets. It's loud, but it doesn't hurt.

Lab draw

A small sample of blood the care team checks to see how the body is doing.

Anesthesia

Special sleepy medicine that helps a child rest and feel nothing during surgery.

Reviewed for plain-language accuracy; always defer to your child's care team for medical advice.
